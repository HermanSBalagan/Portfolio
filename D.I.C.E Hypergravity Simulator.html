Gravity Gizmo: Hypergravity Clinostat System

The Gravity Gizmo is a compact, cost-effective clinostat system designed to simulate hypergravity and microgravity conditions for 3D printing applications. Developed as part of a capstone engineering project, it integrates robust mechanical design, environmental monitoring, and real-time data collection into a safe, scalable platform for space-like experimentation.

Mechanical Architecture

The structure is built with lightweight T-slotted aluminum extrusions to maximize rigidity while keeping the footprint within a 2ft x 2ft constraint. Carbon steel hollow shafts allow stable rotation of the internal frame, which houses a 12kg 3D printer. A NEMA 34 stepper motor with 8 N·m of torque powers the rotation, delivering precise control up to 120 RPM.

To transmit motion, a chain and sprocket system was selected for its balance between reliability and cost. The rotating enclosure is protected by a clear plexiglass casing, enhancing safety without compromising visibility during operation.

Electrical and Control System

The control system features an Arduino-compatible Elegoo board, a microstep driver, and a power supply module. Slip rings mounted on the shaft ensure uninterrupted electrical connectivity to sensors and onboard systems during rotation. The system is designed for intuitive remote access and real-time adjustment of speed and environment.

Sensor and Monitoring Features

Real-time environmental control is achieved using integrated sensors: a DHT22 for humidity, TMP36 for temperature, and an ADXL345 accelerometer for gravity monitoring. A high-resolution camera provides 1080p footage at 30 fps to track 3D printing behavior during operation.

  • ADXL345: 3-axis accelerometer for G-force monitoring
  • DHT22: Combined humidity and temperature sensor
  • TCRT5000: Optical sensor for rotational speed tracking
  • Slip Rings: Enable electrical continuity during motion

Robust Power Delivery

The 8 N·m torque NEMA 34 motor delivers consistent performance, integrated with microstepping for smooth startup. A steel motor plate and precision alignment ensure minimal vibrations during high-speed operation.
